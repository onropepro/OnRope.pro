## Opening Section: The Problem

Most rope access operators know exactly what happens when the auditor asks for six months of harness inspections.

The truck gets searched. The binder gets dug out. Someone says "I think Dave had that folder." Half the forms are missing. The ones you find have coffee stains and illegible handwriting.

You pass the audit. Barely. And you promise yourself you'll get organized.

Until next time, when the exact same scramble happens again.

Meanwhile, your insurance carrier wants proof of your safety culture. Your property manager wants documentation before every job. And if someone gets hurt, the first question a lawyer asks is: "Can you prove you conducted the required inspections?"

Paper forms can't protect you. Filing cabinets can't protect you. The best technicians in the world can't protect you if there's no record of what they did.

## Solution Section: What This Module Does

**Every Harness Inspection. Every Day. No Exceptions.**

Before any technician starts a work session, they complete a digital inspection on their phone. Webbing, buckles, stitching, D-rings, labels. Pass or fail recorded for each item. Photo attached if there's wear or damage worth documenting.

The system timestamps everything. You know who inspected what equipment, when, and whether it passed.

If it fails? The equipment gets flagged for retirement automatically. No one "forgets" to pull it from service.

**Toolbox Meetings That Actually Happened**

Choose from 20+ rope access safety topics: fall protection, rescue procedures, anchor inspection, weather assessment, emergency protocols. Document what you covered. Record who attended with digital signatures or checkbox confirmation.

One meeting covers work within 7 days. The system tracks coverage automatically, so you never wonder "did we do a meeting for this project?"

**Audit-Ready PDFs in Four Clicks**

When the IRATA auditor calls, you don't panic. You log in. You search by date range, technician, or equipment serial number. You export professional PDFs with embedded signatures, timestamps, and all the details regulators expect to see.

The same PDFs go to insurance carriers. The same PDFs go to property managers who want proof before authorizing work. The same PDFs protect you if anything ever goes to court.

## Features Section

**Equipment Inventory**
Track every harness, lanyard, descender, ascender, carabiner, rope, and anchor. Serial numbers, manufacturers, purchase dates, service life remaining. Know exactly what you own and where it is.

**Daily Inspection Checklists**
Digital forms guide technicians through every required check. Binary pass/fail for each item. Notes field for anything that needs attention. Photos for documentation.

**Automatic Flagging**
Equipment that fails inspection gets flagged immediately. Retirement workflows trigger automatically. Unsafe gear doesn't stay in rotation because someone forgot to pull it.

**20+ Safety Topics Library**
Fall protection systems. Weather assessment. Rescue procedures. Emergency protocols. Anchor inspection. Rope condition. Edge protection. Load calculations. Pick the topics relevant to each job.

**Digital Signatures**
Attendance at toolbox meetings verified with legally binding signatures. Timestamps captured automatically. Records become immutable once signed.

**7-Day Coverage Window**
One toolbox meeting covers work within 7 days in either direction. The system tracks coverage automatically, so you always know if you're compliant before starting work.

**Searchable History**
Find any inspection by date, technician, equipment serial number, or project. No more digging through boxes. No more "I think Dave had that."

**PDF Export**
Professional documentation for IRATA audits, insurance carriers, property managers, and legal defense. One click generates everything you need.

## A/B Test Recommendations

**Test 1: Fear vs. Relief Headlines**
- Version A: "The IRATA auditor is here. Are you ready?" (fear/urgency)
- Version B: "Every inspection. Every meeting. Every signature. Searchable in seconds." (relief/capability)
- Why: Different operators respond to different emotional triggers. Some are motivated by fear of consequences, others by vision of competence.

**Test 2: Long-form vs. Condensed Problem Section**
- Version A: Full narrative about the audit scramble
- Version B: Bullet points hitting the pain points directly
- Why: Test whether operators want empathy or efficiency in how problems are presented.

**Test 3: CTA Language**
- Version A: "Start Your Free Trial"
- Version B: "See It In Action"
- Version C: "Get Audit-Ready"
- Why: Different action frames may resonate differently. Trial implies commitment; demo implies evaluation; benefit-focused implies outcome.
