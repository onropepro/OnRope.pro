## 3. What This Module Does

### Section Header

**What This Module Does**

Complete equipment tracking from purchase to retirement.

### Intro Copy

The Gear Inventory module provides centralized equipment management using a slot-based availability model: Available = Quantity - Assigned. Serial numbers are optional metadata for individual unit tracking, not gatekeepers for availability.

### Card 1: Centralized Equipment Database

Track every piece of equipment your company owns in one place. Add items with pre-loaded rope access brands and models (Petzl, CMC, Kong, ISC). Enter quantities, prices, serial numbers. See real-time availability instantly.

**What gets tracked:**
- Equipment type, brand, model, quantity
- Purchase price (visible only to users with financial permissions)
- Serial numbers (optional per item)
- Date of manufacture and in-service dates

### Card 2: Assignment Management

Know who has what at all times. Assign equipment to employees with complete tracking. Employees can self-assign from available inventory or managers can distribute gear directly.

**What gets managed:**
- Equipment assignments by employee
- Serial number tracking per assignment
- Assignment history with timestamps
- Return and retirement workflows

### Card 3: Team Visibility

See your entire company's gear distribution at a glance. The Team Gear view shows every employee and their assigned equipment. Verify crew readiness in seconds, not hours.

**What gets visible:**
- Complete kit status per employee
- Available inventory for new assignments
- Equipment gaps before job starts
- Service life status with color-coded indicators

## 5. Key Features

### Intro Copy

Six capabilities that transform equipment chaos into organized accountability.

### Feature 1: Slot-Based Availability

Available = Quantity - Assigned. This formula is absolute. Add 10 harnesses, assign 4 to employees, 6 remain available. Serial numbers are optional metadata, not gatekeepers. Track individual units when needed; skip serials when you don't.

### Feature 2: Pre-Loaded Equipment Database

Every rope access brand already loaded. Petzl, CMC, Kong, ISC, and more. Select equipment type, pick brand and model, enter quantity. No manual data entry for specifications. Compare this to typing everything from scratch in a spreadsheet.

### Feature 3: Dual Assignment Paths

Managers assign equipment to employees during onboarding or controlled distribution. Employees self-assign from available inventory as needed. Both methods create complete assignment records automatically.

### Feature 4: Financial Protection

Equipment prices visible only to users with financial permissions. Employees see quantities and assignments. Owners see dollar values for insurance, budgeting, and tax purposes.

### Feature 5: Service Life Tracking

Date of manufacture and in-service dates per assignment. Color-coded indicators (green, yellow, red) for hard gear (5-year guideline) and soft gear (10-year guideline). Data for decision-making without automatic retirement.

### Feature 6: Atomic Serial Registration

Enter new serial numbers during assignment. System validates, registers, and creates assignment in one operation. Add serials on the fly as equipment is distributed. Reduce administrative overhead while maintaining complete tracking.

## 10. FAQs

**"What if I have 10 harnesses but only registered 5 serial numbers?"**

You still have 10 available slots. Serial numbers are optional metadata, not gatekeepers. The slot-based model counts quantity, not serial registrations. Add serials gradually as equipment is distributed.

**"Can two different items have the same serial number?"**

Yes. Serial numbers must be unique within each gear item type, but a harness and a rope can both have serial "HR-001." Different manufacturers use similar numbering schemes. The system prevents duplicates within an item type, which is what matters for tracking.

**"What happens when equipment expires?"**

The system tracks in-service dates with color-coded indicators for review. Hard gear (5 years) and soft gear (10 years) guidelines are displayed. You decide when to retire based on inspections and manufacturer recommendations. OnRopePro provides data for decision-making; actual retirement decisions should involve qualified safety professionals.

**"Who can see equipment prices?"**

Only users with financial permissions. Regular employees see quantities and assignments but not dollar values. Financial data is sensitive. Owners need it for budgeting and insurance. Employees don't need to know what their harness cost.

**"Can I delete a serial number that's assigned to someone?"**

No. Return the gear assignment first, then delete or edit the serial. This prevents orphaned assignments pointing to non-existent serials. Data integrity is maintained by enforcing proper return workflow.

**"What if I need to reduce quantity below what's assigned?"**

You cannot. If 5 items are assigned, you cannot reduce quantity below 5. Return some assignments first. This prevents negative availability and ensures the system always reflects physical reality.

**"Do employees need permission to assign gear to themselves?"**

No. All employees can self-assign from available inventory and remove their own assignments. No special permission required. This reduces administrative burden while maintaining complete tracking.

**"How does equipment get added to inventory?"**

Navigate to inventory, add item, select equipment type, pick brand and model from pre-loaded database, enter quantity. Optionally add serial numbers and assign to employees during the same workflow. One-time setup, then the system maintains itself.

**"What's the difference between 'Manage Inventory' and 'Assign Gear' permissions?"**

Manage Inventory allows adding new gear items, editing quantities, and registering serial numbers. Assign Gear allows distributing equipment to other employees. Owners have both. Supervisors might have Assign Gear without Manage Inventory, depending on your workflow.

**"Can employees see what gear coworkers have?"**

Only with View Team Gear permission. Otherwise, employees see only their own kit. Owners and supervisors typically have Team Gear visibility. Regular techs typically don't.
