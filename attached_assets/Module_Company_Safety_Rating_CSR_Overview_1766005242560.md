## ðŸ“‹ Penalty Categories

### Documentation Penalty (Max 25%)

**What's Tracked:**
- Certificate of Insurance (COI)
- Health & Safety Manual
- Company Policy

**How It Works:**
These three documents bring your score from 75% to 100%. Missing any of them keeps you at the lower baseline.

---

### Project Documents Penalty (Max 25%)

**What's Tracked (per project):**
1. **Anchor Inspection** - Verification of anchor points before use
2. **Rope Access Plan (RAP)** - Detailed plan for rope work
3. **Toolbox Meeting** - Pre-work safety discussion
4. **Field Level Hazard Assessment (FLHA)** - Site-specific hazard identification

**Important Exception:**
Anchor inspection is waived for non-rope jobs (parkade pressure washing, ground-level work). The system recognizes when anchor inspection doesn't apply.

**Validated Quote (Lines 243-261):**
> "Anchor inspection, rope access plan, toolbox meeting and FLHA... I need to change the required anchor inspection document for jobs that are just like parkade pressure washing. You're not on rope."

---

### Harness Inspection Penalty (Max 25%)

**What's Tracked:**
- Completion rate of harness inspections across all work sessions
- Each work day should have a corresponding inspection before starting work

**How It Calculates:**

The penalty is a straight percentage of missing inspections:
- 1 miss out of 1 session = 100% penalty in this category
- 1 miss out of 100 sessions = 1% penalty
- 20 misses out of 100 sessions = 20% penalty

**Key Insight:** The math is simple division. As your company accumulates more work sessions, each individual miss has proportionally less impact. This rewards consistent, long-term safety compliance while not destroying your rating for occasional lapses.

**Validated Quote (Lines 275):**
> "Once you have like a thousand sessions, if you're missing one, it won't impact you all that much... I don't want anybody to have 100 because nobody is a hundred percent safe."

---

### Document Review Penalty (Max 5%)

**What's Tracked:**
Unsigned employee acknowledgments for:
- Health & Safety Manual
- Company Policy
- Safe Work Procedures
- Safe Work Practices

**How It Works:**
When employees haven't signed required documents, this category shows the gap. The penalty is proportional to how many employees are missing signatures.

## ðŸ“– Terminology & Naming

### Core Terms

**CSR (Company Safety Rating)**
- **Definition:** A penalty-based compliance score from 0-100% measuring a company's safety documentation and practices
- **Example:** "Our CSR is 87%, down from 92% because two new hires haven't signed the safety manual yet"
- **Why it matters:** Single metric that makes safety culture visible and measurable

**FLHA (Field Level Hazard Assessment)**
- **Definition:** Site-specific hazard identification completed before starting work at a location
- **Example:** "Before starting the facade inspection, complete the FLHA identifying anchor points and fall hazards"
- **Why it matters:** Required project document that identifies job-specific risks

**RAP (Rope Access Plan)**
- **Definition:** Detailed plan for how rope access work will be conducted at a specific site
- **Example:** "The RAP for this building specifies primary and backup anchor points on the rooftop"
- **Why it matters:** Required documentation for any rope access project

**Toolbox Meeting**
- **Definition:** Pre-work safety discussion held before starting a work session
- **Example:** "Morning toolbox meeting covered the construction noise on floors 5-7 and required hearing protection"
- **Why it matters:** Daily safety communication required for each project

### Common Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Full Term | Meaning |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| **CSR** | Company Safety Rating | Overall safety compliance score |
| **COI** | Certificate of Insurance | Proof of liability insurance coverage |
| **FLHA** | Field Level Hazard Assessment | Site-specific hazard checklist |
| **RAP** | Rope Access Plan | Detailed rope work procedure document |
| **H&S** | Health & Safety | General term for safety-related items |

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### "Why does my score start at 75% instead of 100%?"

**Answer:** The 75% starting point reflects that a new company hasn't yet proven their safety documentation is in place. Uploading your three core documents (COI, Health & Safety Manual, Company Policy) immediately brings you to 100%.

**Why this design:** It incentivizes completing basic setup quickly and shows property managers that documentation is verified, not assumed.

---

### "Why did my score drop after I added Safe Work Procedures?"

**Answer:** When you add new documents that require employee signatures, your score temporarily decreases until all employees have reviewed and signed them. Once everyone signs, your score returns to full.

**Why this design:** Adding a document without ensuring employees read it provides no real safety benefit. The temporary drop ensures documents are actually acknowledged, not just uploaded.

---

### "Can I ever reach a perfect 100% score?"

**Answer:** Yes, but maintaining it long-term is very difficult by design. Missing even one harness inspection out of thousands of sessions will slightly reduce your score.

**Why this design:** "Nobody is 100% safe. That would just be a lie." (Line 275) The system acknowledges that perfection is impossible while rewarding consistent compliance.

---

### "Who can see my company's CSR?"

**Answer:** Company owners see full detail. Technicians can see company scores when evaluating employers. Property managers see scores in their vendor dashboard. Building managers and residents cannot see CSR.

**Why this design:** CSR is a vendor selection tool for property managers and a management tool for owners. Building managers handle logistics, not vendor selection. Residents would have concerns without context.

---

### "How does one missed harness inspection affect my score?"

**Answer:** It depends on your total work session count. If you have 1 session and miss 1 inspection, that's 100% penalty in the harness category. If you have 1,000 sessions and miss 1, that's only 0.1% penalty.

**Why this design:** Long-term consistent compliance matters more than occasional lapses. New companies feel the impact more, incentivizing good habits from the start.

---

### "What if I do non-rope jobs that don't need anchor inspection?"

**Answer:** The system recognizes job types. Non-rope work (parkade pressure washing, ground-level tasks) doesn't require anchor inspection, so missing it won't affect your score for those projects.

**Why this design:** Penalties should reflect actual safety requirements. Requiring anchor inspection for ground-level work would be nonsensical.
