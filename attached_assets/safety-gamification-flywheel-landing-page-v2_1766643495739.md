## 2. The Problem We're Solving

### Section Header: Safety Theater vs. Safety Culture

Most rope access companies talk about safety. Some have binders full of procedures sitting in the office. A few actually follow them.

The problem is nobody can tell the difference.

A technician interviewing at two companies hears the same pitch: "We take safety seriously." One company does daily harness inspections, holds real toolbox meetings, and makes sure everyone understands the procedures. The other company hands you gear and says "don't fall." Both claim the same thing. Both look the same on paper.

A property manager evaluating bids sees the same safety claims in every proposal. Everyone has a "comprehensive safety program." Nobody can prove it. Price becomes the only differentiator.

Meanwhile, the companies actually investing in safety get no reward for their investment. The cowboys underbid them and win contracts. Good technicians can't distinguish serious employers from pretenders until they're already on the job.

**This system protects nobody.**

OnRopePro changes the equation by making safety measurable, portable, and visible. Not safety claims. Safety actions. Documented, timestamped, aggregated into scores that follow technicians and companies everywhere they go.

## 3. Two Scores That Change Everything

### PSR: Personal Safety Rating

**What It Measures:**
Your individual safety record, aggregated across every employer you've ever worked for on OnRopePro.

- How often you complete harness inspections
- How quickly you acknowledge and sign safety documents
- Your compliance rate over time, not just today

**Why It Matters:**

Your PSR is portable. It belongs to you, not your employer. When you apply for a new job, employers see your safety rating before they hire you. A tech with a 94% PSR tells a different story than a tech with a 67% PSR.

**The Career Impact:**

Technicians with strong PSR become more valuable. They demonstrate professionalism. They show they take their career seriously. In an industry where your life depends on following procedures, employers want to hire people with a track record of actually following procedures.

**Validated Quote:**
> "There's a safety rating based on how many times they did their harness inspection over time, over all the companies they worked for. An overall how many times they've seen and signed documents and how many times they've completed their harness inspection every day."

### CSR: Company Safety Rating

**What It Measures:**
Your company's safety culture, calculated from operational data across four categories:

| Category | Weight | What Gets Tracked |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| Core Documentation | 25% | Certificate of Insurance, H&S Manual, Company Policy |
| Project Documentation | 25% | Toolbox Meetings, Anchor Inspections, Rope Access Plans, FLHAs |
| Harness Inspections | 25% | Daily equipment inspection completion rate |
| Document Acknowledgment | 5% | Employee signature rates on safety procedures |

**Why It Matters:**

CSR is visible to property managers in their vendor dashboard. When they compare bids, they're not just comparing price. They're comparing 86% versus 43%. Green badge versus red badge.

**The Business Impact:**

Companies that invest in safety finally get credit for it. Companies that cut corners get exposed. The market rewards the behavior we want to encourage.

**Validated Quote:**
> "This is massive. That tells, yes, it tells the property manager that you're a safe company, but it tells you as a company owner how safe you are. How safe is your business? How safe are your employees' knowledge?"

## 4. The Flywheel: How Gamification Transforms an Industry

### Stage 1: Technicians Start Caring About Their Score

When PSR follows you everywhere, you start paying attention. That harness inspection you used to skip when you were "in a hurry"? It affects your score. That safety document you never bothered to read? Employers can see you didn't sign it.

Suddenly, safety isn't something the boss nags you about. It's your professional reputation. It's career capital.

### Stage 2: Employers Hire Based on PSR

A technician applies with a 92% PSR. Another applies with a 58% PSR. Same certifications. Similar experience. Which one do you trust with your company's reputation?

Employers start filtering for high-PSR candidates. The message spreads: if you want the good jobs, maintain your rating.

### Stage 3: High-PSR Techs Raise Company Standards

When you hire people who take safety seriously, your company culture shifts. They do their inspections without being reminded. They sign documents promptly. They hold each other accountable.

Your CSR rises because your team is actually doing the work.

### Stage 4: Property Managers Choose High-CSR Vendors

Two bids on the table. Similar pricing. One vendor shows 91% CSR. The other shows 52% CSR. The property manager has liability concerns. The board asks questions. Insurance auditors want documentation.

The high-CSR vendor wins. Not because they talked better. Because they proved they're better.

### Stage 5: Market Pressure Forces Industry Improvement

Companies losing bids to high-CSR competitors have a choice: improve or keep losing.

The cowboys who undercut on price while cutting corners on safety find themselves locked out of quality contracts. The serious operators who invested in safety culture finally see that investment pay off.

**The industry standard rises. Not because of regulation. Because the incentives aligned.**

## 8. The Gamification Mechanics

### Why Scores Work

Humans respond to measurement. When something is tracked and visible, behavior changes.

**PSR creates individual accountability.** Every technician knows their actions affect their rating. Skip an inspection? Your score drops. Sign documents promptly? Your score rises. The game is clear.

**CSR creates organizational accountability.** Company owners see exactly where they're losing points. The dashboard doesn't just show the score; it shows what to fix. Clear path to improvement.

**Visibility creates market pressure.** When property managers can see the scores, companies can't hide behind marketing claims. When technicians can see employer scores, companies can't fake culture.

### Proportional Impact (Not Punitive)

One missed inspection doesn't tank your score if you have 1,000 clean sessions behind you. The system rewards consistent long-term compliance over occasional perfection.

New accounts feel more impact because they haven't built history yet. This incentivizes good habits from the start.

### Real Improvement, Not Gaming

You can't fake a harness inspection. The system requires it before work sessions can start. You can't fake a signature. It's timestamped and logged.

The scores reflect reality because the underlying actions are enforced by system design.

## 11. FAQs

**"What if I've never used OnRopePro before? Do I start with a zero PSR?"**

New technicians start building their PSR from their first inspection. There's no penalty for being new. Your score reflects your actual history on the platform.

**"Can I fake a harness inspection just to keep my score up?"**

No. The system requires you to step through each equipment category. Failed items automatically retire in inventory. The process takes 30 seconds when done honestly. Gaming it would take longer and create equipment management problems.

**"What happens to my PSR if my employer has a low CSR?"**

Your PSR is independent. It tracks your individual compliance, not your employer's overall score. A disciplined tech working for a disorganized company still builds strong PSR through their own actions.

**"We're a new company. Won't we be at a disadvantage with a short history?"**

New companies can reach 100% CSR immediately by uploading core documents and maintaining daily compliance. History matters for weathering occasional lapses, but new companies with disciplined practices quickly build competitive scores.

**"Do property managers see individual technician ratings?"**

No. Property managers see company CSR only. Individual PSR is visible to employers during hiring, not to external parties.

**"What if I disagree with my CSR calculation?"**

The dashboard shows exactly where you're losing points. If documentation shows 85% but you believe you're at 100%, you can see exactly which documents are missing signatures. The system doesn't hide its logic.

**"This seems like surveillance. Is it?"**

It's accountability. The same way a pilot's flight hours and certifications are tracked, your professional safety actions are tracked. This protects you when things go wrong. It builds your reputation when things go right. Your data belongs to you and travels with you.
