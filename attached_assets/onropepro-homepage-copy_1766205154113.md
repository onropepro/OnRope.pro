## Design & UX Notes

### Segment Selector Behavior

1. **Default state:** "Rope Access Company" is pre-selected (primary audience)
2. **Click behavior:** Selected button highlights, reveals segment-specific hero content
3. **Scroll behavior:** Segment-specific content animates into view; rest of page is universal
4. **Mobile:** Stack buttons vertically or use dropdown

### Visual Hierarchy

1. **Hero:** Large, bold. Selector prominent. Minimal clutter.
2. **Segment content:** Appears below selector. Three-column value pillars.
3. **Problem/Solution:** Three-column comparison. Scannable.
4. **Features:** 4Ã—4 grid with icons. Module names bold.
5. **ROI:** Big numbers. Tables that speak for themselves.
6. **CTAs:** Repeated at hero, after features, and at bottom.

### Copy Principles Applied

- **Clarity = Conversion:** No jargon without context. Every benefit quantified.
- **Identity-first:** "Built by a Level 3" appears early and often.
- **Loss aversion:** Money leaks section before features.
- **Anchoring:** "$34.95 = 1 hour of 1 tech" reframes price perception.
- **Risk reversal:** Free trial, no contract, no credit cardâ€”everywhere.
- **You, not we:** Copy focuses on their problems, their outcomes.

### A/B Test Recommendations

1. **Selector vs. Tabs:** Test horizontal button row vs. tabbed interface
2. **Pre-selection:** Test "Rope Access Company" pre-selected vs. no default
3. **Hero headline:** "One Platform. Every Stakeholder." vs. "Stop Chasing Your Business."
4. **Problem section:** Three columns vs. single scrolling timeline
5. **CTA copy:** "Start Free Trial" vs. "See It In Action"
